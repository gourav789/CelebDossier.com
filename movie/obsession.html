Overview

Obsession is a supernatural horror film that turns a sweet-sounding wish into a waking nightmare. It marks the feature directing debut of Curry Barker, who also wrote and edited the movie.

The story follows a shy young man who longs for his best friend to love him back. When a strange novelty toy grants that exact wish, the results are anything but romantic.

The film became a breakout showcase for its lead actress Inde Navarrette, whose fearless performance drew loud praise from critics and horror fans alike.

Blending dark comedy, heartbreak, and pure dread, Obsession asks a simple question: what happens when you get everything you asked for, and it destroys you?

The Story

Light plot setup ahead — no major twists revealed.

Baron "Bear" Bailey is a gentle, hopeless romantic who works at a small music store. He spends his days with a close circle of friends and quietly carries a big crush on his childhood friend and coworker, Nikki Freeman.

Bear is too nervous to tell Nikki how he feels. He worries that confessing his love could ruin their friendship and make things awkward with their tight group.

One day he picks up an odd little charm called the "One Wish Willow." On a whim, he wishes that Nikki would love him more than anyone else in the world.

The wish comes true almost instantly. But the warm, funny, independent Nikki that Bear adored is slowly replaced by a clingy, intense, and frightening version of herself.

As Nikki's devotion curdles into something dangerous, Bear realizes the wish did not give him love. It gave him a curse, and there may be no way to take it back.

Critical Reception

Obsession arrived to strong reviews after its festival debut, with critics calling it a smart, unsettling twist on the classic "be careful what you wish for" idea.

Much of the praise went to Inde Navarrette, whose shift from charming friend to terrifying force became the talk of the film. Her work sparked early awards conversation among reviewers.

Reviewers also highlighted the movie's blend of dark humor and real emotion, noting that it stayed scary without losing its human heart.

Production & Casting

The film is the first feature written and directed by Curry Barker, who built a following with his short horror work before stepping up to a full movie.

For the role of Nikki, the team auditioned many actresses. Inde Navarrette won the part after Barker felt she perfectly balanced the character's sweet and scary sides.

Michael Johnston was cast as Bear following a chemistry read with Navarrette, and the pairing became the emotional core of the film.

The project was backed by a well-known horror producing team, with Jason Blum serving as an executive producer.
