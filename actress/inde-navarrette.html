Introduction

Inde Navarrette is a young American actress and model who has quickly become one of the most talked-about new faces in Hollywood. In just a few years she has moved from small supporting parts to leading a film that critics and fans could not stop discussing.

Many viewers first met her as Sarah Cushing in the superhero drama Superman & Lois. That role ran for four seasons and turned her into a familiar, trusted presence for television audiences who watched her character grow up on screen.

Before that, she had already reached a huge streaming audience through her role in 13 Reasons Why. These early credits gave her the experience and confidence that would later power her jump to the big screen.

Her true breakout came with the horror film Obsession. Playing a character who shifts from warm and funny to genuinely terrifying, Inde delivered a performance that drew praise for both its emotional depth and its raw intensity. Almost overnight, she went from a recognizable television actress to a rising film star.

That single role changed the shape of her career. Her social media following jumped into the millions, fashion houses and casting directors took notice, and entertainment writers began describing her as one of the most promising performers of her generation.

Early Life

Inde was born Danielle Fabiola Navarrette on March 3, 2001, in Tucson, Arizona. She grew up in a warm, multicultural home that blended two very different cultural backgrounds.

Family Background

Her father is of Mexican heritage and served in the United States Marine Corps. Her mother, Tossi Fox, was born in Australia and works as a hairstylist. This mix of Mexican and Australian roots gave Inde a unique cultural identity that she carries with pride today.

Because her father was in the military, the family relocated frequently while she was growing up. Moving from place to place meant new schools, new friends, and constant change, but it also taught her how to adapt quickly and read new environments, skills that would later prove useful in acting.

Childhood & The Name "Inde"

Rather than seeing all that moving as a burden, she used each fresh start as a chance to reinvent herself and grow. During one of these relocations she adopted the nickname "Inde," a nod to her strong, independent personality. The name stuck, and it is the one she uses professionally to this day.

Friends and interviewers often describe her as expressive, curious, and unafraid to speak her mind, traits that were clearly present long before she stepped in front of a camera.

Heritage & Identity

Inde has spoken warmly about her Mexican roots, and that heritage added personal meaning to some of her later roles. Being able to bring authentic cultural experience to characters has become an important part of how she approaches her work.

Education & Path Into Acting

Because of the family's frequent moves, Inde attended several different schools during her childhood and teenage years. Along the way, her interest in performing kept growing.

She began building her craft through short films and small screen projects as a teenager, gradually turning a childhood love of storytelling into a professional pursuit. By her late teens she was auditioning steadily, and those early efforts soon opened the door to her first real roles.

Career

Inde Navarrette's career is a steady climb built on patience and hard work. She did not become an overnight sensation. Instead, she spent years learning her craft in smaller roles before a single film turned her into a household name among entertainment fans.

Career Beginnings

Inde started out with short films and small screen work in her late teens. She appeared in the short films Cross Words Together (2018) and Cranberry Nights (2020), projects that gave her early camera experience and the chance to test her range.

In 2019 she landed one of her first recurring roles as Veronica in the digital comedy series Denton's Death Date. Made for a social streaming platform, the show gave her valuable practice at carrying a character across multiple episodes.

Breakthrough Roles

A major step came in 2020 when she joined the fourth and final season of the drama 13 Reasons Why as Estela de la Cruz. Estela was a tricky, emotionally loaded character connected to the show's most controversial figure, and playing her placed Inde in front of one of the largest streaming audiences in the world.

The role proved she could handle heavy material and hold her own beside an established ensemble, including actress Alisha Boe. It marked the first time many viewers took notice of her name.

Television Career

Her biggest television role arrived in 2021 with Superman & Lois, where she starred as Sarah Cushing. The series ran through 2024, and across four seasons Inde got to grow a character from a guarded teenager into a young woman dealing with love, family strain, and mental health.

Sarah quickly became a fan favorite because she felt real. On the show, Inde worked alongside a large ensemble led by Tyler Hoechlin and Elizabeth Tulloch, learning from seasoned performers while carrying major emotional storylines of her own.

Film Career

Inde's film journey started in 2020 with the emotional drama Wander Darkly, a small but meaningful early feature credit. In 2025 she took a supporting role in the action thriller Trap House, showing she could work in a completely different genre.

Then came Obsession, the supernatural horror film that made her a star. As Nikki Freeman, she anchored the entire movie, and her transformation from charming friend to frightening threat became the film's biggest talking point. The role premiered at a major film festival before reaching wide audiences, and it repositioned Inde as a genuine leading actress.

Character Analysis

What connects Inde's best-known roles is emotional honesty. As Sarah Cushing, she played vulnerability and strength at the same time, making a teenage character feel grounded rather than dramatic. As Nikki Freeman, she took that same emotional truth and pushed it into darker territory, so that the horror felt believable because the character felt human first.

This ability to keep a character relatable, even when the story turns extreme, is a big reason critics have singled her out. She tends to build performances from real feeling rather than surface effect.
